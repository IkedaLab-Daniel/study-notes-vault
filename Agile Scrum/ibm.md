# Lesson 1: Introduction to Agile

## What is Agile?

Agile starts with one simple question:

> **How can I get better today?**

Agile helps individuals, teams, and businesses improve by changing the way they work and collaborate. It focuses on creating a culture of continuous improvement through specific values, principles, and practices.

---

# Being Agile vs Doing Agile

## Being Agile
Being Agile means developing an **Agile mindset** — a way of thinking and approaching work.

An Agile mindset is:
- **Described by five values**
- **Defined by three principles**

## Doing Agile
Doing Agile means applying those values and principles through practices and methods such as:
- Iterations
- Lean
- DevOps
- Design Thinking

### Flow of Agile
```text
Mindset → Values → Principles → Practices
```

---

# History of Agile

## Why Agile Started

Agile emerged because traditional software development methods were often too slow and inflexible.

## Key Milestones

### Late 1990s — Pre-Agile Era

Developers searched for better ways to build software.

### February 2001 — Birth of Agile

The **Agile Manifesto** was created.

---

# The Agile Manifesto

The Agile Manifesto emphasizes:

* **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools
* **Working software/products** over comprehensive documentation
* **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation
* **Responding to change** over following a plan

---

# Evolution of Agile

Agile continued evolving throughout the 21st century by integrating ideas from:

* Lean Methodology
* Design Thinking
* Neuroscience

# Agile Values, Principles, and Practices

## Definition of Value

A value is something considered important or worthwhile.

In Agile:

* Values are not just beliefs
* Values must be practiced daily

### Important Idea

> The values you ignore are also your values.

Example:

* If you see litter and walk past it, you have decided it is not important enough to act on.

In Agile teams:

* Team members must actively live the Agile values
* Values shape team behavior and culture

---

# IBM Agile Values

IBM highlights five core Agile values:

1. Trust
2. Empathy
3. Respect
4. Openness
5. Courage

---

# 1. Trust

## Definition

Trust is the willingness to be vulnerable with others.

It creates a safe environment where people can:

* Make mistakes
* Ask for help
* Take risks
* Speak honestly

### Key Insight

> Vulnerability builds trust.

### Practice Examples

* Admit mistakes openly
* Ask teammates for help when needed

---

# 2. Empathy

## Definition

Empathy is understanding another person’s perspective, even when you disagree.

It promotes:

* Cooperation
* Team commitment
* Better communication

### Practice Examples

* Listen carefully during disagreements
* Ask thoughtful, non-judgmental questions
* Try to see situations from others’ perspectives

---

# 3. Respect

## Definition

Respect means treating people with dignity and valuing their contributions.

Respect encourages:

* Diverse thinking
* Better ideas
* Inclusive teamwork

### Practice Examples

* Give everyone a chance to speak
* Value all opinions equally

---

# 4. Openness

## Definition

Openness means:

* Having an open mind
* Listening without judgment
* Being transparent with the team
* Being willing to try new ideas

### Practice Examples

* Listen actively to others
* Share your thoughts honestly
* Ask questions to better understand viewpoints

---

# 5. Courage

## Definition

Courage is speaking up and taking action despite fear or difficulty.

It means:

* Sharing different ideas
* Addressing problems openly
* Standing by your values

### Practice Examples

* Suggest alternative solutions
* Speak up when something is not working
* Share ideas even if they differ from the group
